Curtis McGinity

Scholar. Consultant. Trainer. Athlete.

Curtis earned his PhD in Optimization, creating new statistical inference techniques for adaptive machine learning algorithms to be applied in the real-world, in real-time, on humans.

His dissertation work focused on optimizing clinical trials, challenging how clinicians and regulators balance the tradeoff between information gain and patient safety.

Curtis is now focused on the project of internalizing the optimality of solutions within daily life, building intelligence into systems that augment life in material, and mathematically optimal, ways.

Specifically, Curtis is building a decade's worth of self-experimentation and systems optimization into cutting edge technologies for:

  • nutrition regimens,
  • supplement stacks,
  • fitness programming,
  • personalized pharmaceuticals.

Curtis writes on a variety topics centered on realizing the frontiers of your best possible lifestyle at The Art of Optimal. You can learn more about his research on the interface of machine learning and statistical inference at his academic website.
